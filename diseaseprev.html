Disease Prevention Guide

Essential disease prevention strategies from Sebaste Community Hospital

Prevention Strategies for a Healthier Life

In today's world, staying healthy is more important than ever. Whether you're at home, at work, or traveling, practicing a few simple habits can make a big difference. Here are four essential tips to keep you and your loved ones protected from illness all year round.

Proper Handwashing Techniques to Reduce Infection

Handwashing is one of the easiest—and most effective—ways to stop the spread of germs. Here's how to do it right:


  • Wet your hands with clean, running water.
  • Lather with soap, covering the backs of your hands, between your fingers, and under your nails.
  • Scrub for at least 20 seconds (hum the "Happy Birthday" song twice!).
  • Rinse under clean running water.
  • Dry with a clean towel or air dry.

Always wash your hands after coughing or sneezing, using the restroom, before eating, and when returning home.

Vaccination Schedules for All Age Groups

Vaccines protect you from serious diseases and help prevent outbreaks in your community. Here's a quick guide:


  • Infants & Children: Polio, MMR, DPT, Hepatitis B, Rotavirus
  • Teens: HPV, Meningococcal, Tdap booster
  • Adults: Annual flu shot, COVID-19 boosters, Hepatitis A/B, Pneumococcal
  • Seniors: Shingles, updated Pneumococcal vaccines, necessary boosters

Consult your healthcare provider at Sebaste Community Hospital for a vaccination plan tailored to your age and health condition.

Seasonal Illness Prevention Strategies

Each season brings different health risks. Here's how you can stay prepared:


  • Rainy Season: Eliminate stagnant water to prevent mosquito-borne diseases like dengue.
  • Cold Season: Get your flu shot and eat nutrient-rich foods to boost immunity.
  • Hot Season: Stay hydrated, wear breathable clothing, and avoid midday sun exposure.

Regular check-ups and staying aware of seasonal symptoms go a long way in prevention. Visit our clinic at the first sign of unusual symptoms.

Travel Health Precautions

Going on a trip? Keep these health tips in mind:


  • Before Travel: Visit our travel clinic for destination-specific vaccines and advice.
  • During Travel: Use hand sanitizer, eat safe food, drink bottled water, and protect against insects.
  • After Travel: Monitor for symptoms for 2-4 weeks and seek medical help if needed.

Bring a basic first-aid kit and stay updated with travel advisories from local health authorities.

"Your health is your greatest wealth. Practice these habits every day to live well and stay safe."
