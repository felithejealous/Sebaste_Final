Sebaste Ramps Up MR-bOPV Special Immunization Activity for Children Under Five

Sebaste, Antique — In its continued commitment to protect children from preventable diseases, the Municipality of Sebaste launched the Measles-Rubella and bivalent Oral Polio Vaccine Special Immunization Activity (MR-bOPV SIA) this May, targeting children aged 0 to 59 months across various barangays.

The activity kicked off on the first week of May in the border barangays of Callan and Aguila, marking Day 1 of the campaign. The RHU emphasized the urgency of the initiative, citing the serious risks posed by Measles, Rubella, and Poliomyelitis, which remain among the most common vaccine-preventable childhood illnesses.

The vaccination is completely free and will be available throughout the entire month of May, aiming to reach as many eligible children as possible. Parents and guardians are strongly encouraged to bring their children to the nearest vaccination site to ensure protection and immunity against these life-threatening diseases.

On Day 2, the MR-bOPV SIA continued in Barangay Aras-asan, where the activity was successfully carried out with the support of MDRRM personnel, the Sebaste Municipal Police Station (MPS), and the Barangay LGU. Their cooperation highlights the strong inter-agency effort behind the program.

The campaign is part of the #ChikitingLigtas2023 initiative, a nationwide push to close the immunization gap and strengthen community health.
